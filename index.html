About Us

Our Story

We did not begin in a high-tech aerospace lab. We did not have million-dollar equipment. We did not have NASA engineers mentoring us. We were just ambitious individuals living on a small island in Vietnam, whose dreams were to reach beyond the stars.

We started with a seemingly impossible question: “If plasma always forms around spacecraft during re-entry… why don’t we control it to our advantage?” Yet from that vague idea, Photon-Shaper was born: a system that uses high-power lasers, beam-shaping optics, and magnetic fields to create a lightweight, controllable, active plasma shield - without needing extra fuel.

The Problem We Are Solving

  • Drag wastes fuel.
  • Heat destroys materials.
  • Blackout disconnects the crew from Earth.
  • No existing technology solves all three - unless plasma can be actively shaped.

Scientific Background

  • Femtosecond lasers can reduce drag by up to 50% at Mach 3.
  • Strong magnetic fields can reshape re-entry plasma flows.
  • Beam shaping can control plasma geometry in real time.
  • Photon-Shaper combines all into a compact, scalable system with commercial potential.

Technology

How Photon-Shaper Works

  • Lasers to generate plasma filaments.
  • Optical beam-shaping lenses to compress or widen the plasma shape.
  • Magnetic coils to “bend” plasma like a sculptor shaping clay.
  • Control algorithms to form a stable, adjustable “plasma dome.”

Why It’s Revolutionary

  • Active Plasma Shaping: Controlling its geometry for efficiency.
  • Non-Mechanical Steering: Create directional forces to steer without wings.
  • Energy Efficiency: Wide plasma distribution – lower power consumption vs. localized heating.
  • Patent-Ready Design:
    • Beam-shaping optical system.
    • Plasma geometry control algorithms.
    • Integrated plasma-dome architecture.

Research

Validation Approach

  • Existing evidence: Laser-generated plasma reduces drag significantly.
  • Available hardware: Laser modules, magnets, SLM optics are already commercial.
  • Proven physics models: Lorentz-force plasma shaping has been demonstrated.

Planned Experiments (8-Month Roadmap)

  1. Wind Tunnel Testing: Measure drag, shock structure, and flow-field changes with plasma activated.
  2. RF Blackout Testing: Test communication through plasma layers in an anechoic chamber.
  3. Thermal Shielding Testing: Measure heat flux reduction and surface temperature with plasma dome.

Current Progress

  • A full experimental framework.
  • Device architecture and blueprints.
  • Measurement protocols.
  • Safety plan and validation criteria.
  • A complete 8-month execution roadmap.
